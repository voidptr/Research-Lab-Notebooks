Research thoughts (11:50a)

  • think about whether the display A/B increased by performance of tasks

    • limited resources (niching) gets you divergence of display A/B and prefer A/B

    • non-limited does not.

  • Does this sufficiently allow for speciation based on drift?

    • Do we want niching? The speciation between benthic and limnetic stickleback was not sympatric, but geographic.

    • So, no niching. Just geographic separation. Maybe use limited resources, because all environments contain limited resources

    • My gut is that tasks hitting only two display traits is not enough

      • Why? Why do I feel that?

      • First of all, because half of the time, they may randomly land on the same task, where if there are more options, the chance of randomly landing on the same task is lower.

    • So, you also want them to evolve separately long enough under different conditions such that they DO NOT want to blend their species when they are rejoined.

    • Also, concerned about the populations having to compete with each other when they rejoin. So, do we want niching to prevent the populations from just wiping one another out?
