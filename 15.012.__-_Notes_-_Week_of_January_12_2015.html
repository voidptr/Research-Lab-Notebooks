Proposal Text

[Figure - geneflow between allopatric populations with sexual selection] Previously, we have shown that Avidian digital organisms with sexual selection will speciate in allopatry, and have significantly reduced geneflow when rejoined in sympatry. Now, we will use Avida to explore the mechanisms and effects of reinforcing selection on sexually-selecting digital organisms evolved in allopatry and sympatry. Specifically, we will evolve populations of organisms that use sexual selection to choose mates on the basis of varied traits, with variable strength of choosyness on those traits. Then we will split the populations into separate environments and allow them to diverge for different lengths of time. The split populations will then be rejoined in sympatry at different time points. First, we hypothesize that the organisms in allopatry will have lower choosyness strength, whereas the mingled populations will evolve to be more strongly choosy on their traits. Second, we hypothesize that the early mechanism producing reinforcing selection will be based more strongly on avoiding sexually unfit hybrids, whereas at later times, reinforcing selection will be based on avoiding hybrid inviability.
